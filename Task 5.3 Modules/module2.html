Common Tools and Equipment in Joinery

Understanding the correct use and safety measures for each tool is essential for producing quality work safely.

Tool Primary Uses Key Safety Measures
Powered Plane Taking shavings off timber to make it flat, smooth, or to reduce its thickness. Check blade sharpness. Ensure workpiece is securely clamped. Keep hands clear of the blade area.
Cordless Drill General purpose drilling of holes and driving screws. Ensure battery is charged. Use the correct bit for the job and ensure it's secure in the chuck. Maintain a stable stance.
Chisels Removing timber to create joints (like mortises or housings) and for fine-tuning fits. Always keep both hands behind the cutting edge. Keep the chisel sharp. Wear safety glasses to protect from flying chips.
Router Creating trenches or grooves for joints, shaping edges, and trimming laminates. Check cord for damage. Clamp workpiece securely. Keep fingers clear of the spinning bit. Unplug when changing bits. Use dust extraction. Wear safety glasses and earmuffs.
Sliding Compound Mitre Saw Cutting timber to length at various angles (typically from 90 to 45 degrees) for mitre joints and other angled cuts. Keep hands well clear of the blade's path. Use the blade guard. Clamp the workpiece securely against the fence. Wear safety glasses and earmuffs.
Claw Hammer Driving and removing nails. Ensure the handle is secure and free of cracks. Keep fingers clear of the point of impact. Wear safety glasses.
