Module 3: Planning and Preparing for Safe Work Practices

3.1 Site Induction and Training

Preparation starts on day one at a new site or project with a site induction. An induction is a safety orientation that familiarises you with the specific workplace, its hazards, rules and emergency procedures. Under WHS law, every worker – including work experience students – must complete a site‑specific induction before commencing work, and PCBUs must provide this training and consult workers about safety matters. In construction you also need to have completed the general construction induction (White Card) and any task‑specific training before starting work.

3.2 Work Plans and Documentation

  • Job Safety Analysis (JSA) or Safe Work Method Statement (SWMS): Documents that list each step of the job, the hazards associated with it, and how those hazards will be controlled. For high-risk construction work, an SWMS is mandatory.
  • Drawings and Specifications: Check any plans or drawings to identify hidden hazards (e.g., services, structural elements) before starting.
  • Permits and Approvals: Ensure permits (hot work, confined space, etc.) are in place as required.
  • Identify Resources: Verify correct tools, equipment, and trained personnel are available before work begins.

3.3 Planning the Task – Step by Step

  • Clarify the Task Requirements: Understand work instructions and quality standards to avoid unsafe shortcuts.
  • Identify Hazards: List hazards specific to your task using knowledge from earlier modules.
  • Decide on Controls: Determine elimination, substitution, engineering, administrative, or PPE controls for each hazard.
  • Consult and involve others: Discuss your planned controls with your supervisor and the work team. WHS legislation requires PCBUs to consult with workers when identifying hazards and deciding on risk control measures; consultation helps ensure controls are practical and effective.
  • Sequence the Work: Plan the order of steps to maintain safety throughout.
  • Gather Equipment: Select correct and inspected tools, then secure any faulty equipment with tag-out procedures.
  • Communicate the Plan: Conduct a toolbox talk so everyone understands hazards and controls.
