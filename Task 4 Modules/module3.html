Accurate estimation is what wins jobs and makes a profit. This involves identifying all the materials and labour required and calculating their cost.

Key Terms and Processes

  • What is a "Take-Off"? This is the process of determining the quantity, quality, and then the cost of each material needed to complete a job. You carefully review project plans and "take off" all the necessary materials to form a list for costing.
  • What is a Quantity Surveyor? A quantity surveyor is a tertiary qualified professional who specialises in building measurement and estimates the value of construction costs. They are experts in preparing detailed cost reports to help manage project budgets.
  • Where do you find material information? A materials list is where you can find a summary of materials required for a construction project. This list is often included on the project plans.

How Building Materials are Sold

Understanding the unit of sale is crucial for correct ordering.

  • By Lineal Metre (m): For materials where only the length matters.
    • Examples: Framing timber, Skirting Board, Cornice.
  • By Square Metre ($m^2$): For materials that cover a surface.
    • Examples: Laminate Flooring, Pavers, Tiles, Plaster Board.
  • Individually (Each/Per Item): For single units or pre-cut sheets.
    • Examples: Pavers, Plywood, Tiles, Yellow Tongue Flooring sheets, Plaster Board.
  • By Volume (Cubic Metre, $m^3$): For bulk materials.
    • Example: Ready-mixed concrete.
  • By Litre (L): For liquids.
    • Example: Paint, sealants, adhesives.
