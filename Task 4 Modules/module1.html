This module will help you build a strong foundation in the mathematical skills that are used on-site every day.

Unit Conversions: Millimetres (mm) and Metres (m)

In Australia, construction plans use millimetres (mm) for precision, but materials are often sold in metres (m). You need to be comfortable converting between the two.

  • The Key Relationship: 1000 mm = 1 m
  • Converting mm to m: To convert from millimetres to metres, you divide by 1000.
    • Example: $5200 \text{mm} \div 1000 = 5.2 \text{m}$
  • Converting m to mm: To convert from metres to millimetres, you multiply by 1000.
    • Example: $8.5 \text{m} \times 1000 = 8500 \text{mm}$

Calculating Area (Square Metres - $m^2$)

Area tells you the amount of surface to be covered, which is essential for ordering materials like flooring, plasterboard, or turf.

  • Rectangles and Squares: Used for floors, walls, and concrete slabs.
    • Formula: Area = Length × Width
    • Example: A room that is 4.5m long and 3.0m wide has an area of $4.5 \times 3.0 = 13.5 m^2$.
  • Circles: Used for circular patios, garden beds, or pillar bases.
    • Formula: $A = \pi \times r^2$ (Area equals Pi times the radius squared).
    • Remember: Pi ($\pi$) is a constant, approximately equal to 3.14. The radius (r) is the distance from the centre of the circle to its edge; it is half of the diameter.
    • Example: A circular slab with a radius of 2m has an area of $\pi \times (2 \times 2) = 12.56 m^2$.
  • Complex Shapes (like Trapeziums): Often, the easiest way to find the area of a complex shape is to break it down into simpler shapes you know how to calculate, like rectangles and triangles. Calculate the area of each simple shape and then add them together.

Calculating Volume (Cubic Metres - $m^3$)

Volume tells you the total space inside a three-dimensional object. This is critical for ordering materials like concrete or soil. Important: Ensure all your measurements are in the same unit (metres) before you start calculating volume.

  • Cubes and Rectangular Prisms: Used for concrete footings, blocks, or trenches.
    • Formula: Volume = Length × Width × Height (or Depth)
    • Example: A concrete footing 5m long, 0.5m wide, and 0.6m deep has a volume of $5 \times 0.5 \times 0.6 = 1.5 m^3$.
  • Cylinders: Used for round concrete piers or post holes.
    • Formula: $V = \pi \times r^2 \times h$ (Volume equals Pi times the radius squared, times the height).
    • Example: A pier hole that is 1.5m deep with a radius of 0.2m requires $V = \pi \times (0.2 \times 0.2) \times 1.5 = 0.188 m^3$ of concrete.

Calculating Labour Costs and GST

  • Labour Cost: To find the total cost for a worker, you multiply their hourly pay rate by the number of hours they worked.
    • Formula: Labour Cost = Hourly Rate × Hours Worked
  • Adding GST: The Goods and Services Tax (GST) in Australia is 10%. To add GST to a total, you can multiply the total by 1.10.
    • Formula: Total including GST = Total Cost × 1.10
