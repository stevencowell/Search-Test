üîπ Maintaining and Replacing Components

  • Circular Saw ‚Äì Blade Replacement: Disconnect power. Use a spanner or locking button to hold the blade. Loosen the retaining bolt, remove old blade, fit new blade with correct rotation direction, tighten and test before use.
  • Power Planer ‚Äì Blade Replacement: Disconnect power. Loosen blade housing screws or release lever. Remove old blades and fit new ones evenly. Secure tightly and ensure balance across the drum.
  • Router ‚Äì Bit Replacement: Disconnect power. Use two spanners or a locking button to loosen the collet. Remove old bit, insert new bit to correct depth, and tighten securely. Double-check seating.
  • Angle Grinder ‚Äì Disc Replacement: Disconnect power. Lock the disc with a spanner, loosen the nut. Replace disc with correct type. Check rotation direction and tighten nut securely.

These tools become dull over time and need regular sharpening. The sharpening process involves grinding (restoring the edge) and honing (finishing the edge).

  1. Grind the edge using a bench grinder or wet stone:

  2. Flatten the back of the blade on a sharpening stone:

  3. Hone the bevel on a sharpening stone:

üîπ Cleaning and Storage

  • Wipe tools clean ‚Äì remove dust, glue, or shavings.
  • Apply oil to steel surfaces if tools are not used regularly.
  • Store tools properly ‚Äì return to designated racks, drawers, or shadow boards.
  • Label damaged tools and report immediately.
  • Power tools should be unplugged or have batteries removed, with cords coiled neatly and stored off damp surfaces.

By performing these maintenance and sharpening tasks, you extend the life of your tools and ensure they are ready for use when you need them. Sharp, well-maintained tools make your work easier and produce higher-quality results. Moreover, using a dull tool can be dangerous‚Äîit often requires excessive force and can slip.
