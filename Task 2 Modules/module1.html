üîπ Leveling and Straight Line Tools

Spirit Level
Used to ensure horizontal (level) and vertical (plumb) surfaces. Handle with care to avoid breaking the glass vials. Less effective for long-distance levelling.

String Line
Creates a long, straight reference line between two points. Useful for laying bricks or posts. Must be kept taut and clear of foot traffic to avoid trip hazards.

Chalk Line
Used to snap a straight, visible line across a surface. Ideal for laying out framing or sheet material. Eye protection is recommended to avoid chalk dust.

üîπ Cutting and Shaping Tools

Handsaw
Used to manually cut timber. Reliable but slower than power tools. Teeth can become blunt over time. Workpieces must be secured before use.

Coping Saw
Ideal for cutting curves in thinner timber or ply. The thin blade is flexible and suited to detail work, but care is needed to avoid snapping it.

Chisel
Used to shape timber, especially for joints like housings or rebates. Keep hands behind the blade and always chisel away from your body. Sharpen frequently.

Hand Plane
Used to smooth or reduce the thickness of timber. Push with the grain for best results. Must be kept sharp and flat for clean, even finishes.

Tin Snips
Used for cutting thin sheet metal. Always wear gloves and eye protection to avoid injury from sharp edges. Not suitable for thick or rigid metal.

üîπ Fixing and Assembly Tools

Claw Hammer
Used for driving or pulling nails. Grip it near the base for better control and impact. Avoid damaging surfaces when removing nails.

Trimming Knife
Used for cutting soft materials like insulation or plasterboard. Blades must be changed regularly. Always cut away from your body and keep fingers clear.

Clamps
Used to hold workpieces steady during cutting, drilling, or gluing. Choose the right size clamp and avoid overtightening. Position to keep work stable without blocking your cutting line.

Crowbar / Fencing Bar
